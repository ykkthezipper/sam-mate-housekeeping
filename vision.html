        Keep the full vision, sequence the build

        Next pilotConnected start of shift

        Supervisor assignment, worker start screen, optional reminders and a clearer hand-off to existing tools.

        Needs company sign-in, an approved data store and operational ownership.
        IntegrationRooms, routes and Grounds linen flow

        Read-only room data, editable laundry counts, restock load planning, route ordering and replenishment forecasts.

        Needs system-owner approval, supported data access, room/zone data and operational validation.
        Portable workflowSAM and Take Five terminology

        Use the local name while preserving task, hazard, controls and worker review.

        CCC and JHA remain separate workflows until their critical-risk rules and approvals are understood.
        ResearchTest before committing

        On-device PPE assistance, fair-allocation suggestions, assisted hazard reports, team tips, recognition and live inventory.

        These stay in the vision but need separate privacy, governance, integration and evidence reviews.

        What each stage should prove

        Useful before bigger

        Safety
        People understand the hazard and controls.
        Usability
        The workflow reduces friction without removing review.
        Operations
        Later integrations save real walking or planning effort.
        Non-negotiable boundaries
        • No passwords, real staff names or internal maps in the public prototype.
        • No automatic form submission or hidden performance scoring.
        • No algorithm replaces supervisor or HSEQ judgement.
        • Only approved SWP / SWSP content enters the shared safety library.
